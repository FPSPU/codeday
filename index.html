Welcome to #PoliHack 2 at the YWCA!

The Ontario Youth Strategy rallies partners from across multiple sectors to improve outcomes for youth. The Strategy focuses on providing equitable and targeted supports for sub-populations of youth (e.g Indigenous youth, newcomer youth) who face multiple barriers to success.

Your mission, if you choose to accept it, is to use your expert knowledge and make this strategy more user focused.

    • Challenge 1 - Theory of Change - Stepping Stones

    A Resource on Youth Development


    Stepping Stones provides up-to-date research and information about youth development to guide the delivery of high quality supports and services for youth aged 12 to 25 across Ontario. It is designed to help anyone who works with or cares for youth to identify and respond to their needs at each stage of their development

    Problem Statements

    1. How do we adapt Stepping Stones, a document designed to help anyone who works with - or cares for - youth, for a service provider working on the frontline?

    2. How can we implement Stepping Stones from a user experience perspective?

    Readings

  • Stepping Stones Report
  • Early Adolescence (12–14 years old) Developmental Map
  • Adolescence (13–19 years old) Developmental Map
  • Early Adulthood (17–25 years old)
  • Stepping Stones Posters and Postcards

    • Potential Hack Projects

  • Mapping YOW risk and protective factors to Stepping Stones developmental framework (e.g. early adolescence, adolescence, early adulthood) [Policy Issue: Evidence Based Services]
  • Stepping Stones Developmental Maps quick reference app / web app for youth worker or parent [Policy Issue: Knowledge Mobilization]
  • Mapping your agency’s strategic goals / framework to Stepping Stones developmental framework [Policy Issue: Evidence Based Services]

    • Core Materials

  • Current YOW Data Entry Interface

    • Challenge Area 2: Strategic Policy Framework - Stepping Up

    A Strategic Framework to help Ontario’s Youth Succeed


    Stepping Up establishes a strategic framework to help guide, focus and maximize our collaborative actions to support young people. At its core is a set of 20 outcomes that can help all of us – service providers, foundations, community groups, governments, young leaders and families – better align our work with what research and youth themselves say is important for their success. It is a basis for MCYS sustained, collective action in support of Ontario's young people.

    Problem Statement

    How can we help (insert your organization name here) better align with the priority outcomes of Stepping Up?

    Readings

  • Stepping Up Executive Summary
  • Stepping Up Full Document
  • Stepping Up Annual Report 2015
  • Stepping Up Annual Report 2014

    • Potential Hack Projects

  • Tool to determine a youth agency’s organizational type and key Stepping Up themes services are provided under [Policy Issue: Evidence Based Services, Asset Mapping]
  • Creating a front end to a database of Ontario youth agencies by organizational type and Stepping Up themes services, location and contact information

    • Core Materials

  • SWOT Analysis
  • List of Youth Agencies

    • Challenge Area 3: Monitoring Impact - Ontario’s Profile of Youth Wellbeing

    The Profile of Youth Well Being is based upon the Stepping Up framework. The goal of the Profile is to give a snapshot on how Ontario Youth are doing.

    Problem Statements

    1.How can we better understand how young people are doing in Ontario?

    2. How can we wireframe the Profile of Youth Well Being to make it more immerse, interactive and dynamic

    Readings

  • 2013 Profile of Youth Wellbeing
  • 2014 Profile of Youth Wellbeing
  • 2015 Profile of Youth Wellbeing

    • Potential Hack Projects

  • Identifying and mapping local community indicators to the Outcomes Framework in the Profile of Youth Wellbeing (e.g. digging deeper into the difference between the provincial graduation rate and the graduation in a community) [Policy Issue: Evidence Based Policy Development]
  • Identifying and mapping at-risk and high-risk sub population (e.g. disaggregated data related to Indigenous youth, LGBTTQ youth) indicators to the Outcomes Framework in the Profile of Youth Wellbeing (i.e. digging deeper into the difference between the provincial graduation rate and the graduation rate for racialized youth) [Policy Issue: Evidence Based Policy Development]
  • Researching and identifying currently missing indications and statistics and how to go about collecting this data

    Challenge Area 4: Targeted Investments

    The Government of Ontario has a number of initiatives that support the wellbeing of youth across all seven Stepping Up themes. These initiatives and programs are stored in lists and difficult to dig up from the ministry website. Help us design a tool that better connects the services MCYS provides with the people who truly need them

    Problem Statement

    How can we make youth services easier to find in your local communities?

    Readings

  • Inventory of youth programs / services
  • Enhanced Ontario Youth Action Plan Program Providers

    • Potential Hack Projects

  • App / web app that allows you to search government-funded youth services within a geographic radius [Policy Issue: Service Navigation]
  • Intake-style questionnaire app / web app that recommends youth services based on youth needs (can be linked to Stepping Stones developmental maps) [Policy Issue: Needs Based Service Delivery]
  • Youth needs by at-risk and high-risk subpopulations (e.g. Indigenous youth, racialized youth, newcomer youth, etc.)
  • Searchable web map of all government-funded youth services by keyword, location or geographic boundary [Policy Issue: Service Navigation]
